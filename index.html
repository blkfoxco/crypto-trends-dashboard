How it works

What you're seeing

Stacked area chart of relative Google search interest for 15 crypto terms over 5 years. Bitcoin's USD price is overlaid as a white line on a logarithmic right-hand axis. Click legend entries to show/hide individual terms.

Data sources

  • Google Trends — weekly search interest via the unofficial pytrends API
  • BTC Price — weekly close from Binance BTCUSDT spot (no auth required)

Cross-batch normalisation

Google Trends returns scores of 0–100 relative within a single query. Since max 5 terms can be queried at once, we batch in groups of 4 with Bitcoin as a shared anchor. Each batch's Bitcoin series is compared to the first batch's Bitcoin baseline to derive a scale factor, letting all 15 terms share a common y-axis.

BTC price axis

BTC price is plotted on a linear scale (right axis, USD), making absolute dollar moves easy to read at a glance.

⚠️ Google Trends represents relative search popularity, not absolute volume. The anchor normalisation approximates cross-batch comparability but may introduce minor distortions where Bitcoin's own trend line differs between batches. Use as a directional signal, not a precise metric.
